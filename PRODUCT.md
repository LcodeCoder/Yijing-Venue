# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Players who enjoy compact tactical card games in a desktop or mobile browser. They need to understand the current turn, legal actions, card effects, battlefield ownership, and AI responses at a glance without memorizing hidden rules.

## Product Purpose

Field Realm is a single-player tactical card game about deploying sites and units, contesting an interconnected battlefield, and outmaneuvering an AI opponent. Success means that every action is readable, every legal choice is discoverable, and strategic decisions about hand management, positioning, range, and site control produce visibly different outcomes.

## Brand Personality

Tactical, mystical, and responsive. The interface should feel like a living arcane game board: deliberate rather than noisy, dramatic at important moments, and clear enough that presentation never obscures rules.

## Anti-references

Avoid static spreadsheet-like card tables, unexplained pop-up-only feedback, generic dashboard styling, excessive decorative motion, hidden legal-action rules, placeholder or corrupted text, and effects that delay play without communicating state.

## Design Principles

1. Show cause and effect: deployment, drawing, combat, retreat, discarding, and range changes must be spatially and visually connected.
2. Make legal actions obvious: selection, valid targets, invalid targets, costs, capacity, and attack range should be visible before commitment.
3. Reserve spectacle for meaningful events: draw reveals and clashes may be dramatic, while routine controls remain fast and restrained.
4. Keep the battlefield legible: ownership, adjacency, unit slots, and current phase outrank decoration.
5. Preserve tactical agency: players should be able to revise deployments, manage dead cards, and understand why the AI acted.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.1 AA where practical. Do not rely on color alone for ownership or targeting; pair color with icons, labels, borders, and motion. Maintain keyboard focus visibility and usable touch targets. Respect `prefers-reduced-motion` by replacing long choreography with immediate state changes and concise fades.
